Research Interests

The Pandey Lab studies the molecular mechanisms of ALS and related motor neuron diseases. Using Drosophila and mammalian models, the lab investigates how RNA-binding proteins such as TDP-43 and FUS contribute to disease pathology. The lab also focuses on GEMIN5, a key player in RNA metabolism, and its role in neurodevelopmental disorders.

Research

We explore the molecular mechanisms of ALS and motor neuron degeneration using fruit flies, iPSC-derived neurons, and mouse models. Our lab investigates the role of RNA-binding proteins like TDP-43, FUS, and GEMIN5 in RNA metabolism and neurodegeneration.
